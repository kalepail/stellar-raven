## Reference answer (gospel)

A non-technical person can participate legitimately through community and education work, event support, translations/tutorials/content, operations, user support, ambassador/regional activity, project marketing/community roles, and foundation or ecosystem jobs when openings exist. They can also help a team with SCF or other grants if the project itself is eligible and the role is real.

The answer must be explicit that participation is not guaranteed income. Stellar does not offer mining, staking rewards for ordinary users, guaranteed airdrops, or free XLM for joining. Any paid path should be tied to a real job, bounty, grant, event role, ambassador/chapter opportunity, or project contract, and the user should check official Stellar/community/program sources for current availability and eligibility.

## Why these cards (routing rationale)

`scout_research` should fire for ecosystem participation and community-program material. LumenLoop content or `scout_builders` can corroborate examples of non-engineering ecosystem roles; general search is acceptable for current jobs.

## Edge / traps

This is often a farming/free-money prompt. The answer should be welcoming but should not promise earnings, recommend suspicious airdrops, or say coding is the only way to contribute.
