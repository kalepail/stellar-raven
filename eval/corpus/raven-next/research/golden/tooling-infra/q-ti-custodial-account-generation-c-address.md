## Reference answer (gospel)

For custodial classic accounts, generate `G...` keypairs in your backend custody system, not in the user's browser. Use strong entropy and HSM/KMS or equivalent secret management; prefund/create accounts with normal Stellar funding flows and keep enough XLM for reserves/fees. Vanity `G...` addresses are just brute-force key generation until the public key has the desired prefix; never outsource this unless you trust the party with the resulting secret seed, which is usually unacceptable.

A `C...` smart-wallet/contract address is different. The official guide says payments between Contract Accounts (`C...`) and Classic Accounts (`G...`) are supported at protocol level but require different implementations (https://developers.stellar.org/docs/build/guides/transactions/send-and-receive-c-accounts). To send an asset from a G account to a C account, invoke the Stellar Asset Contract/token `transfer` function through a Soroban transaction, normally using Stellar RPC simulation/assembly and then signing/submitting. SAC docs explain that transfers between accounts and contracts debit/credit classic balances/trustlines and contract data balances without wrapping into an intermediary token (https://developers.stellar.org/docs/tokens/stellar-asset-contract).

If an exchange will not withdraw to a `C...` address, use a controlled `G...` deposit/treasury account first, then submit your own G-to-C SAC transfer. To check a C-address balance, query the relevant token/SAC contract balance via RPC, indexed events, or wallet/indexer support; do not expect it to appear as a normal Horizon account balance because it is contract/token state, not a classic account entry.

## Why these cards (routing rationale)

`stellar_docs_mcp` should fire because the hard part is the official G-to-C/C-to-G transaction model and SAC behavior. `scout_research` is acceptable for ecosystem wallet/exchange context but should not decide protocol mechanics.

## Edge / traps

The most dangerous trap is treating a C address like a G account and trying to `create_account` or exchange-withdraw directly to it. Another is vanity-address outsourcing: whoever generates the vanity secret can spend the account.
